Cannabis

Cannabis, marijuana, weed, pot, is a psychoactive drug. It has medical and recreational uses, people have been using it for medical reasons for thousands of years, but now it has been replaced by morphine and other synthetic opioids. Now it’s modern medically used as first-line therapy, I would like to note that there are people that give their kids, who have cancer, cannabis and don’t bring them to chemo, saying it can prevent cancer growth because the active ingredient thc supposedly attacks the cancer cells in the body, but there is no medical research backing this up. There are 0 recorded deaths caused directly by THC, you cannot overdose on it. There have been cases where cannabis causes a reduction of seizures, but there still is no clear proof that it is not a correlation. Although a lot of people believe it, cannabis is not a miracle drug, it has its drawbacks just like every other substance there is a lack of good evidence on both medical risks and therapeutic benefits of marijuana. You can get addicted/form a habit of smoking but not form an addiction to the actual active ingredient THC. Although lawmakers, teachers, and parents like to say weed is a gateway drug, it has been proven by research that it is not and may help cure addiction to other substances like alcohol. Medical research has led some states of the USA and some European countries to legalize it for medical purposes. Since the start of Covid-19, many people have started to use cannabis medicinally for chronic health conditions and as a substitute for other medication.
Dangers: There have been reports of Cannabis causing cardiac arrest and seizures, leading to depression, although there is no solid proof of it not being a correlation. Regular use of cannabis is associated with impaired functioning, decreased academic performance, motivation, and, psychiatric disorders like psychosis, schizophrenia, and mood disorders, though there is still no solid proof. May cause addiction, especially if mixed with tobacco or its leaves.

Caffeine

Caffeine is a stimulant commonly found in Coffee, tea, energy drinks (the common replacement is guarana). It is the drug of choice for almost everyone who lacks sleep and replaces it with coffee in the morning to wake up, its effects are usually felt in 5-30minutes, it increases breathing and heart rates increase mental alertness and physical energy depending on the person it can last up to 12 hours. Caffeine is not a replacement for sleep, although everyone including teachers and schools thinks so, the energy does not appear from nowhere, it is not an unlimited source of energy. As with any drug, you can develop a tolerance. Caffeine and green tea have been proposed as ways to reduce obesity and promote weight loss and to maintain weight. Positive effects using this have been achieved by using green tea mixtures, but a green tea and caffeine mixture has been shown to improve weight management through thermogenesis, fat oxidation, and sparing fat-free mass. Caffeine is also the main ingredient in many weight-loss pills, I suspect it is to promote a faster metabolism, more energy for exercise and other activities. Some studies show that caffeine supplements are beneficial to high-intensity exercise, while others show no change. One study indicated a significantly greater bench press maximum with caffeine and no significant change in other kinds of exercise and a higher blood pressure post-workout.
Dangers: Caffeine is a highly addictive, dependence, and habit-forming drug, especially in high amounts in synthetic versions like what is seen in energy drinks. Withdrawal effects can start to show up even at 3 days of exposure to caffeine. Withdrawal symptoms: headache, fatigue, decreased energy/activeness, decreased alertness, drowsiness, decreased contentedness, depressed mood, difficulty concentrating, irritability, and foggy/not clearheaded, and even some flu-like symptoms, nausea/vomiting, and muscle pain/stiffness.
